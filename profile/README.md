## 中文

# WHY

> "What I cannot create, I do not understand." — Richard Feynman

在 AI 时代，这句话变得比以往任何时候都更刺眼。

今天，Claude 这样的模型已经可以写出世界一流水平的代码和系统设计：
操作系统、数据库、编译器、分布式系统、机器学习框架……
很多我们从来没真正搞懂的东西，AI 一眼就能帮我们造出来。

这既是力量，也是某种无力感。

- 我们在调用巨大的黑盒；
- 我们依赖它们做越来越多的事情；
- 但我们并不知道这些黑盒里面发生了什么。

我想做的事情，是在这种时代背景下，重新回答三个问题：

1. **AI 时代，什么叫真正"理解"一门计算机学科？**
   仅仅会用工具已经不够了。
   我希望自己至少能做到：
   - 知道 AI 在做什么；
   - 知道这些能力依赖哪些最基本的结构、协议和架构；
   - 知道这些基石为什么存在、为什么是这样被构造出来的。

2. **AI 时代，一套"合格"的计算机课程应该教什么？**
   当所有学生手里都握着一个世界级的 AI 助手，
   过去那种"教你手写排序、手写链表"的课程会显得过于简单。
   新的问题变成：

   - 我们到底应该带学生走多深？
   - 哪些部分可以交给 AI，哪些部分必须自己走一遍？
   - 到什么理解深度，我们才敢说"我真的懂了这门课"？

3. **我们如何利用 AI，不是简化学习，而是加深理解？**
   我不想把 AI 当成"跳过困难部分的捷径"，
   而是想当成"放大第一性原理思考能力的机械臂"。
   我希望：

   - 我来提出问题、澄清假设、设计抽象；
   - AI 来帮我用代码和实验把这些抽象推到极限；
   - 最终产出一批从零实现的系统，
     它们既是工具，也是课程，也是理解的证据。

因此，这个项目不是在"复刻传统教材"，
而是在 AI 时代重新构造一套**基于第一性原理的计算机学科基建**：

- 用一个个 from-scratch 的系统，重新理解操作系统、数据库、编译器、网络、分布式、机器学习和搜索；
- 用人类 + AI 的协作方式，探索"理解"这件事在 21 世纪的新含义；
- 顺便为未来的 CS 学习者，提供一条能够匹配 AI 时代的学习路径。

简单说就是一句话：

**我不想只会用工具。
我想知道，这个世界是怎么被搭起来的，哪怕有一天只能靠我和一个 AI， 我也能从零把它重建出来。**

## English

# WHY

> "What I cannot create, I do not understand." — Richard Feynman

In the age of AI, this quote has become more piercing than ever before.

Today, models like Claude can already write world-class code and system designs:
operating systems, databases, compilers, distributed systems, machine learning frameworks...
Many things we never truly understood, AI can help us build in an instant.

This is both power and a certain sense of powerlessness.

- We're invoking massive black boxes;
- We rely on them to do more and more things;
- But we don't know what's happening inside these black boxes.

What I want to do is, in this era, to re-answer three questions:

1. **In the age of AI, what does it really mean to "understand" a computer science discipline?**
   Simply knowing how to use tools is no longer enough.
   I hope to at least be able to:
   - Know what AI is doing;
   - Know which fundamental structures, protocols, and architectures these capabilities depend on;
   - Know why these foundations exist and why they were constructed this way.

2. **In the age of AI, what should a "qualified" computer science curriculum teach?**
   When all students have a world-class AI assistant in their hands,
   the old curriculum of "teaching you to hand-write sorting algorithms and linked lists" seems too simple.
   The new questions become:

   - How deep should we really take students?
   - Which parts can be delegated to AI, and which parts must we walk through ourselves?
   - To what depth of understanding can we dare say "I truly understand this subject"?

3. **How do we leverage AI, not to simplify learning, but to deepen understanding?**
   I don't want to treat AI as a "shortcut to skip the hard parts,"
   but rather as a "mechanical arm that amplifies first-principles thinking."
   I hope:

   - I ask the questions, clarify assumptions, and design abstractions;
   - AI helps me push these abstractions to their limits through code and experimentation;
   - Ultimately producing a collection of from-scratch systems,
     which serve as tools, courses, and evidence of understanding.

Therefore, this project is not about "replicating traditional textbooks,"
but rather reconstructing a **first-principles-based infrastructure for computer science** in the age of AI:

- Using from-scratch systems to re-understand operating systems, databases, compilers, networks, distributed systems, machine learning, and search;
- Using human + AI collaboration to explore what "understanding" means in the 21st century;
- Along the way, providing a learning path for future CS learners that matches the AI era.

Simply put, in one sentence:

**I don't want to just know how to use tools.
I want to know how this world is built, so that even if one day it's just me and an AI, I can rebuild it from scratch.**
